ONDC (Open Network for Digital Commerce) -- Network Policy Overview
Source: ONDC official Resources & Knowledge Base (resources.ondc.org/ondc-network-policy)
This is a summary in original wording of ONDC's own published policy overview.

What ONDC Is

ONDC is a government-backed initiative to "unbundle" e-commerce in India. Instead
of one company controlling the whole value chain (customer acquisition, seller
onboarding, order flow, fulfillment, payments, grievance handling -- the
traditional "platform model"), ONDC lets separate, independent entities plug into
a shared, open network to perform each of these functions. A buyer using one app
(a "Buyer App") can discover and transact with a seller who only ever listed
their catalogue on a completely different app (a "Seller App").

Key Legal Relationships on the Network

- Buyer and Seller: bound by the terms of the sale itself; title to goods
  transfers via the invoice.
- Buyer and their Buyer App: an ongoing relationship formed when the buyer
  signs up.
- Seller and their Seller App: an ongoing relationship formed when the seller
  signs up.
- Buyer App and Seller App: a transaction-specific contract formed for each
  individual order.
- Both apps and ONDC itself: governed by the Network Participant Agreement and
  the Network Policy (ONDC itself is not a party to any individual sale).

Fundamental Principles All Network Participants Must Follow

- Comply with all applicable laws and regulations.
- Act in the best interests of end users and the network as a whole.
- Cooperate with other participants to resolve buyer/seller complaints in a
  timely manner.
- Maintain confidentiality and user privacy in transactions.
- Prohibit illegal or unlawful content on their app.
- Act fairly, reasonably, and without discrimination -- no participant may be
  given preferential treatment over another in the same situation.
- Maintain transparency with end users and other participants.

Structure of the Network Policy (by Chapter)

1. Onboarding, Compliance, and Certification -- eligibility and process to join
   the network, categorised by role (Seller App, Buyer App, or Gateway).
2. Business Rules -- roles/responsibilities of Buyer Nodes, Marketplace Seller
   Nodes, Inventory Seller Nodes, and Gateways; requires non-discriminatory
   treatment of participants.
3. Commercial Model -- defines commission structure and commercial terms
   between participants, aimed at transparency and consumer protection.
4. Code of Conduct and Ethics -- forbids bribery, requires protection of
   confidential information, and prohibits conflicts of interest.
5. Branding Guidelines -- rules for using ONDC's logo, trade name, and
   trademarks.
6. Issue and Grievance Management -- mechanisms for resolving complaints filed
   by buyers, sellers, or other participants.
7. Network Data Governance -- obligations around protecting private and
   personal information.
8. Network Technology Governance -- audit and certification framework to
   verify participants meet technical standards.
9. Governance and Disciplinary Policy -- process for handling non-compliance
   with the Network Participant Agreement or Network Policy.

Why this matters for a chat-commerce order-risk platform

- ONDC formalises exactly the segment of small, unorganised sellers this kind
  of project targets, but ONDC itself only provides the commerce "rails" --
  discovery, ordering, and settlement protocol. It does not provide order
  risk-scoring, RTO prediction, or chat-based order capture.
- A chat-native order intelligence tool is complementary to ONDC, not
  competing with it: a seller could take orders over chat (outside ONDC) and
  separately be discovered via ONDC (inside ONDC) -- both funnel into the same
  underlying seller operations.
- ONDC's own Network Data Governance chapter and non-discrimination principle
  are useful reference points if this project ever needs to formalise its own
  buyer-trust-network data-sharing rules.
